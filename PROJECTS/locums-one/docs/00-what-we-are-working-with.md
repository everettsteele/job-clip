## The business in one paragraph

Locums.One is a physician-owned locum tenens staffing agency. Hospitals pay a bill rate, physicians (1099 contractors) get a pay rate, and the agency earns the spread. The wedge against incumbents (CompHealth, AMN, Aya): a transparent 15–22% markup vs the industry's 40–60%, founder-level continuity instead of recruiter churn, and speed (21-day standard placement, 15–30 day parallel credentialing, 48-hour emergency deploys).

## Unit economics (from chapter 02)

Example: EM physician at $350/hr pay → +10% malpractice ($35) → bill at 20% margin = $481.25/hr → minus ~5.7% platform fees → **~$69/hr net margin**. A single 72-hour assignment ≈ **$5K margin**. Repeat placements with career locums are five-figure relationships — the game is recurring placements, not one-off shifts.

## The BD operating system (how the work gets done)

- **Daily 50** (3–4 focused hrs/day): 25 engagements + 15 new outreaches + 10 follow-ups + 1 content piece; all doctor replies answered same day. "One DM a day is a hobby. Fifty quality reps a day is a business."
- **Core Four lead sources:** warm outreach, content, cold outreach, channels/partnerships.
- **Follow-up cadence:** touches at ~5 days, 2 weeks, 1–2 months — most conversions land after message 2–4.
- **Key-man-risk doctrine:** everything logged in shared trackers with source tags; nothing lives in anyone's head.
- **Diagnostic ladder when underperforming:** verify volume → improve quality → only then expand channels.

## The strategic wedge: new grads (chapter 03)

Reposition locums as a **2–3 year debt-elimination strategy**, not a career. Data: ~$212K average med-school debt, 30%+ locums pay premium ($180/hr ≈ $350K+/yr vs ~$180K employed), only 8% of physicians do locums, 42% report burnout. Nobody recruits new grads for locums — "you're the first person who ever told them." Objection scripts cover malpractice, credentialing, stability, and moonlighting-while-employed.
