## The three that actually move the number

**1. Malpractice is double-treated.** ch.02 builds malpractice *into the bill* ($350 + $35 = $385, then mark up). Marc's email *subtracts* malpractice from the margin. If it's already passed through to the hospital, deducting it again from the commission base is a double-count that quietly costs the rep ~15–20% × $10K = **$1.5K–$2K** per contract.

**2. Platform fees vanished.** ch.02 explicitly drops gross margin to net via a 5.7% platform fee — that's the entire $96.25/hr → $69/hr step. Marc's email never subtracts it. On $500K that's ~$28.5K. Its absence *inflates* the commission base by ~15–20% × $28.5K = **$4.3K–$5.7K** in the rep's favor — so his $88K base is actually *more generous* than the field guide's own net-margin logic, not less. Worth confirming that's intentional and not an omission he'll later "correct" downward.

**3. Reconciled, the two models don't land in the same place.** On an identical $500K bill:
   - Field-guide-consistent net (malpractice in bill, −5.7% platform fees): **$100K − $28.5K = ~$71.5K**
   - Marc's stated base (−malpractice −cred, no platform fees): **$88K**
   - That's a **$16.5K** swing in the commission base from definitional choices alone — before anyone discusses the 15 vs 20%.

## Net read

Nothing here is a "gotcha" — 20% sits inside the public range and his arithmetic is internally clean. The discrepancies are all in **what counts as the base**: malpractice handled as passthrough in one doc and a deduction in the other, the 5.7% platform fee present in the field guide and absent from his email, and "average contract" set ~15–20× above the field guide's own example assignment. Two of those cut for the rep, one against; together they make his $88K base **$16.5K higher** than the field guide's net-margin math would produce — which is good for the rep but means "Net Revenue" needs one fixed definition before it's a commission term.
