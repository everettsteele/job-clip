## Doctor conversation playbook (chapter 13) — the recruiter core

**SMS outreach formula:** greeting → facility → rate & schedule → travel hook → coverage specifics → soft CTA ("Worth a call?") → opt-out. Rules: <160 chars where possible, "Dr. Last" never first name, no $$/em-dashes/emoji (spam filters), 8am–9pm local only.

**Reply triage:** HOT (reply <30 min, call within 24h) · WARM (same day, address the concern) · QUESTION (1 hr, answer then pivot to call) · COLD (graceful close, keep) · STOP (permanent opt-out) · WRONG # (remove).

**Rate negotiation ladder:** within 5% of target → accept. 5–15% over → "let me run it by the team," escalate to Marc with margin math. >15% over → offer ceiling, emphasize covered malpractice/travel, keep warm. One round max; never invent facility caps.

**Discovery call (15–20 min):** frame as long-term fit → background + dealbreakers → licensure map + IMLC eligibility → preferences (call/no-call, solo/team) → practical close (rate floor, start date, 1099 mandatory — W-2 demand is a dealbreaker) → enroll + recap within 1 hour.

## Field notes (chapter 12) — what has actually worked

- **Dr. Khaira (heme/onc):** cold SMS → submission in 7 hours by leading with explicit rate ($575/hr).
- **Dr. Margolis (urology):** prior facility privileges cut credentialing 60 days → 5 days. Warm/reactivated doctors convert 5–10× faster than cold.
- **Dr. Gopaldas (CT surgery):** 44-shift block, $274K bill value — block placements over one-offs.
- **May 2026 audit:** 104 HOT leads, 3.8% HOT→submission conversion, 7.6× ROI on outreach spend, **>30% AR failure rate from facilities canceling jobs** (demand-side fragility — relevant to hospital BD).

## Channels & moats (chapter 14)

Three moats: (1) **non-IMLC state benches** — pre-licensed doctors in CA/NY/VA deploy instantly while competitors wait months; (2) **specialty depth** — premium (interventional cards, GI, heme/onc, CT surgery) + named internal gaps: **psychiatry, neurology, pulm/crit care**; (3) **residency/fellowship pipelines**. Long tail: conferences, military/VA transitions, lapsed locums, visa-waiver docs, CMO relationships.

## Partnerships & Dream 100 (chapters 15, 16, 06)

"Go to whoever already has the customers." Partner types: creators (rev-share, done-for-you assets), perm recruiters (cross-referral splits), communities (sponsorship/AMAs, no vendor spam). Deal structures: flat fee, tiered rev-share, lifetime residuals; 60–180-day attribution windows tagged `partner_<name>`. Competitors (Locums United, Nomad, Medicus) treated as potential overflow partners. Case study: @therealtopdoc (Ali Chaudhary) — one avatar (burned-out employed physicians), one promise ("start locums in 30 days").

## Federal lanes (chapter 07)

Don't bid prime; supply physicians under certified primes: SDVOSB (~$4.5–6.5M sole-source caps), 8(a), and **ANCs (unlimited, non-protestable sole-source — Chenega, Cape Fox)**. Buyers: VA, DoD, IHS, BOP. Action: deepen the Buser/teleradiology lane now, court ANC primes long-term, seed week 9+.

## Glossary essentials

**AR** = Assignment Request (job order, lives on **LocumSmart** VMS) · **Bench** = tagged/tiered doctor database · **IMLC** = compact license in ~7–10 days vs 60–120 · **Prior privileges** = credentialing accelerant · **Source tag** = commission attribution.
