# Commission Follow-up from Marc — Review

2026-06-22. Marc (CFO/BD) sent the commission number our fit assessment said was never stated. This note reviews it against `02-fit-assessment.md` and the field-guide economics in `00`/`01`.

## What Marc said

> We generally pay 15–20% of Net Revenue (after Malpractice and Credentialing) as commission.

Worked example he provided:

| Line | Amount |
|---|---|
| Average contract size | $500,000 |
| Typical Locums.One margin (20%) | $100,000 |
| Malpractice | −$10,000 |
| Credentialing | −$2,000 |
| **Net Revenue (commission base)** | **$88,000** |
| Commission @ 15% | $13,200 |
| Commission @ 20% | $17,600 |

The math is internally consistent: 20% of $500K = $100K; minus $12K = $88K; 15–20% of $88K = $13.2K–$17.6K.

## What this changes

- **It fills the one blank that made the deal unscoreable.** Our pass was "as structured" largely because payout was unknown on a small pool. We now have a number.
- **On the stated average contract, the per-win check is five figures, not four.** Our original estimate ("a four-figure check after three months") assumed a first placement near the field guide's own $5K-margin / 72-hour example. Marc is quoting the *mature/blended* contract, where one win is $13K–$18K. That is materially better than we modeled — **if** the average is real and reachable.

## What it does not change

1. **"Average contract size: $500K" is the load-bearing assumption — and it is not the first win.** The field guide's own chapter 02 example is a 72-hour assignment ≈ $5K company margin. At 15–20%, that first realistic placement is **~$700–$900**, not $13K. A $500K contract is the Gopaldas-scale block / recurring career-locums relationship (their own big example, Gopaldas CT surgery, was $274K bill value — still roughly *half* of $500K). So the new number describes steady state. The gap between the ~$800 first win and the $15K average **is the entire unpaid ramp**, and you carry it.
2. **~250+ unpaid hours before the first dollar still stands.** Marc's note is about rate, not timing. The program's own ramp puts first placement at day 57–90.
3. **The >30% AR cancel rate haircuts expected value.** Commission pays only on completion; if 30%+ of sourced opportunities die to facility cancellations, the $17.6K ceiling is really ~$12K expected per sourced contract.
4. **Hospital BD still has zero comp path.** Marc's entire model is doctor-source attribution ("Net Revenue" on a placement you sourced). The other half of the named seat — hospital-side BD — is uncompensated in this structure, exactly as `02` flagged. This is still the biggest hole.
5. **Relationship ownership is still one-directional.** A higher commission % doesn't change that the bench, pipeline, and close belong to their system by design.

## Putting it in income terms

- To clear ~$100K/year at the $15.4K midpoint, you need ~6–7 completed $500K contracts. After the 30% AR haircut, ~9–10 *sourced* contracts that reach completion — solo, in a year that doesn't pay its first dollar until ~day 60–90.
- In first-placement units instead: ~$800/short assignment means 100+ of those for the same $100K. The model only works on the block/recurring bull case, which is precisely the part that takes the longest to build.

## Revised verdict

The number is good news and worth saying so — it moves the *upside* case from "unknown small" to "five figures per mature contract." It does **not** move the *structural* verdict in `02`: commission-only, deferred 60–90 days, attrition-discounted, with no comp for the hospital-BD half of the role and one-directional relationship ownership.

## Two questions back to Marc (these decide it)

1. **What is "average contract size"** — per placement, per doctor relationship lifetime, or per year? And what does the *first* sourced placement typically pay a new rep in months 3–6, not the blended average?
2. **How is the hospital-BD side compensated?** The model shown only recognizes doctor source tags.

The credible counters from `02` are unchanged and now have a price to anchor against: **retainer + commission** for the BD seat, or **Merkava-as-vendor** running their BD function as a paid service.
