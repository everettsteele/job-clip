# Fit Assessment — Pass (as structured)

2026-06-12. Everett's call after reading the full guide: not a good fit as offered. This note records why, so the reasoning survives if the conversation with Locums One continues.

## The deal as offered

Recruiter + hospital BD support. Commission-only on completed placements of doctors tagged to you. No base, no retainer. Commission percentage is never stated anywhere in the 19-page guide.

## Why it fails on its own numbers

- **~250+ unpaid hours before first possible dollar.** The Daily 50 is 3–4 focused hours/day; the program's own timeline puts first placement at day 57–90.
- **Unknown payout on a small pool.** Margin is ~$69/hr; a 72-hour assignment ≈ $5K company margin. Whatever the commission cut is, the first win is likely a four-figure check after three months of full half-days.
- **The key-man-risk doctrine is one-directional.** Every contact must be logged into *their* system immediately and becomes a company asset by design ("the relationship belongs to the system, not your memory"). Everett would build audience on his own face/LinkedIn, but the bench, the pipeline, and the close all belong to them. They've engineered out dependency on him; his income depends entirely on them.
- **>30% AR cancel rate** (their own field notes): even perfectly sourced doctors lose a third of opportunities to facilities canceling — and commission only pays on completion.
- **Hospital BD has no comp path at all.** The guide's commission mechanism only recognizes doctor source tags.

## The Merkava angle

Automating the Daily 50 through Merkava (content Drive, outreach cadence, follow-up, CRM logging) is technically plausible — but automation lowers the cost of doing unpaid work inside someone else's attribution system. It doesn't fix the deal structure.

The interesting inversion: Locums One *is* the Merkava ICP — operators with a real playbook and no payroll to execute it. If the relationship continues, the credible counters are:

1. **Retainer + commission** for the BD seat, or
2. **Merkava-as-vendor**: Merkava runs their BD function (content, outreach, follow-up, logging) as a paid service, with Everett as operator rather than commission rep.

## Status

Passing on the role as structured. Door open if the deal changes shape.
